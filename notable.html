Proxima Centauri b

Discovered in 2016, Proxima Centauri b is the closest known exoplanet to Earth, orbiting the star Proxima Centauri, the closest star to our Sun. It lies within the habitable zone and could have conditions suitable for liquid water. Its proximity makes it a prime candidate for further study. Understanding this exoplanet could provide insights into the potential for life beyond our solar system.

Kepler-22b

Discovered in 2009, Kepler-22b is located in the habitable zone of its star and is about 2.4 times the size of Earth. It orbits a star similar to the Sun and may have conditions suitable for liquid water, making it a significant target for studying potentially habitable worlds.

Trappist-1 System

The red dwarf star TRAPPIST-1 is home to the largest group of roughly Earth-size planets ever found in a single stellar system. Located about 40 light-years away, these seven rocky siblings provide an example of the tremendous variety of planetary systems that likely fill the universe.

Osiris

Osiris is an exoplanet that has caught the interest of scientists due to its unique features. It orbits within the habitable zone of its star, suggesting the potential for liquid water. Studies suggest that it might have a rocky composition and an atmosphere suitable for life.
